Reading the Interface

An interface is not a neutral window onto content — it is discourse. Its buttons, menus, and layouts quietly build one way of participating into the “correct” one: the path of least resistance. This site lets you use three social platforms and then annotate the interface to surface the norms built into their design.

Reddit’s layout makes evaluation the easiest gesture: the very first control on every post is a vote. Contribution is ranked, comments nest into arguments, and a sidebar of rules plus a Report flag hand governance to the crowd. The interface scripts a deliberative, competitive public whose currency is karma.

Interface 02 · curatorial & affective

The grid puts images first and text last, and the two easy verbs are save and like — you curate rather than create, and your affect is instantly counted. Friz & Gehl show how a sign-up of feminized interest categories scripts a feminine curator; Gerlitz & Helmond show how each like becomes a metric in a like economy. The norm: cooperate, collect, feel — in numbers.

There is no vote and no clean “original” view — the easy move is reblog, which copies a post onto your blog and adds you to a growing chain of notes. Tags act as sideways commentary rather than search terms. The interface scripts a transformative, fannish public where value is circulation: Highfield, Harrington & Bruns’ affective audiencing, made the default gesture.
